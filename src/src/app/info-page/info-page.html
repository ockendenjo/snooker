Pub Snooker

Get the highest score according to the rules of pub snooker

The (white) cue ball is the pint glass, used to pocket the colours in allowable orders

Game play

  1. Start a new break by potting a red (ABV ≤3.8)
  2. (Optionally) Pot a colour (ABV ≥3.9)
  3. (Optionally) continue steps 1 and 2 until pub closing time
  4. After 15 reds, and up to 15 colours have been potted: pot the colours in order
    1. Yellow
    2. Green
    3. Brown
    4. Blue
    5. Pink
    6. Black

Rules

  • Drinks can be consumed in any pubs or beer festivals worldwide
  • Players must consume pub or beer-festival pints
  • Cider, and non-real ale are permitted, home-brew is not
  • Drinks must be consumed with another Trotter who also must be drinking

Cider and AF-beer

  • AF-beer is valid for a red (ABV ≤3.8)
  • AF-beer is not valid for a colour. If AFB must be consumed, submit the valid drink consumed by someone else present
  • AF-beer can be consumed in pints or bottle/can-sized measures.
  • Cider drinkers can choose to submit their drink or the drink of someone else present
  • Cider should be consumed in pints

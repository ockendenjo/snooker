Pub Snooker

Get the highest score according to the rules of pub snooker.

The (white) cue ball is the pint glass, used to pocket the colours in allowable orders.

This challenge is not about drinking the most pints, but the correct pints in the correct order.

Game play

You must pot a red, then a colour if possible, then a red, then a colour; and so on until your 15 reds have been potted (each followed by a colour unless you end the day with a red).

Then, from wherever they are on the table, the final six balls must be potted in order: yellow, green, brown, blue, pink, black (=clearance).

There is no merit in hurrying; it's all about break-building.

Duration

Pub snooker runs from 17 Apr to 03 May 2027 (inc.) which is the same as the 2027 World Snooker Championship. It is equivalent to a frame of snooker, therefore each Trotter quaffs a maximum of 36 pints.

A drinking day resets at 3AM UK time. If you will be taking part from outside the UK please get in contact.

Key rules

  • Drinks can be consumed in any pubs or beer festivals worldwide.
  • Players must consume pub or beer-festival pints.
  • Cider, and non-real ale are permitted, home-brew is not.
  • Drinks must be consumed with another Trotter who also must be drinking.

Cider and AF-beer

  • AF-beer is valid for a red (ABV ≤3.8).
  • AF-beer is not valid for a colour. If AFB must be consumed, submit the valid drink consumed by someone else present.
  • AF-beer can be consumed in pints or bottle/can-sized measures.
  • Cider drinkers can choose to submit their drink or the drink of someone else present.
  • Cider should be consumed in pints.

Scoring

As per snooker. That is: potting colours in the correct order, aiming to maximise one's current break (day's drinking session) and cumulative score (for the championship).

See ABV table for ABV mappings to colours and points.

Fouls

Quaffing in the wrong order is a foul. It ends your break, and you lose points.

  • If the cue ball is potted or leaves the table (i.e. you spill your pint), then you lose 4 points.
  • It you pot a red after a red in the same session without a colour in between, you lose 4 points.
  • Before all the reds are potted, if you pot two colours without a red in between you lose 7 points if the black is involved, 6 for the pink, 5 for the blue, otherwise 4 points.
  • The same applies if you start a break with anything other than a red, whilst any of your reds are still on the table, or if you pot a colour out of order at the clearance stage.

Tactics

A break is a Trotter's amount correctly quaffed during a single day's opening hours, in the correct order until interrupted by a foul stroke, or choosing to stop, or closing time.

Provided the above rules are followed, you may range across any pubs, any ales and any Trotter drinking partners.

As long as any of your reds are on the table, it's efficient to drink an even number of pints in a day, otherwise you sacrifice a colour you could have potted after a red.

The rest is up to you ... ATQB!

Eternal glory

As with Trotter drinking challenges, the prize is taking part. Eternal glory will be awarded in the following categories:

  • Highest individual cumulative score. Anyone will be able to drink their way to a 147
  • Highest break in any one session (i.e. day's opening hours)
  • Biggest clearance at the end (i.e. in an individual's final day's session)
  • Most different pubs visited
  • Most unique beers quaffed
  • Most different beer styles quaffed
  • Most stylish or innovative achievements or methods of play: players may choose to wear bow-tie and waistcoat, or wear white gloves, or munch down beta-blockers, or sing novelty chart hits, or engage in any other more-or-less relevant behaviour for the verisimilitude.
